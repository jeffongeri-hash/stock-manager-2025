The 4% Rule (Safe Withdrawal Rate): Research shows that withdrawing 4% of your portfolio per year has historically survived 30+ year retirements in almost all market conditions. Your FIRE Number = Annual Expenses ÷ 0.04, or Annual Expenses × 25. For a longer early retirement (40-50 years), many FIRE planners use a more conservative 3.5% or 3% SWR.

The Two Phases of Coast FIRE
1
Accumulation Phase — Save aggressively until Coast Number
Invest as much as possible. Every dollar invested now needs to grow to cover your retirement. This phase ends when you hit your Coast number — often much sooner than full FIRE.
2
Coasting Phase — Only cover current expenses
You no longer need to save a dollar. Your portfolio compounds uninterrupted. Work a lower-stress job, part-time, or pursue creative work. The psychological benefit is enormous — financial anxiety largely disappears.
3
Full FIRE — Portfolio reaches target, work is optional forever
At your coast-to age, your portfolio has compounded to your full FIRE number. You can fully retire, keep working because you love it, or draw down the portfolio at your SWR rate.

FIRE Quick Facts
F.I.R.E. = Financial Independence, Retire Early
4% Rule: Withdraw 4%/year from your portfolio. 96% historical success over 30 years.
25× Rule: Save 25× your annual expenses = financial independence.
Savings Rate matters more than income. 50% savings rate → FIRE in ~17 years regardless of income.
Index funds outperform 90%+ of actively managed funds over 20-year periods.
