Sequence of Returns Risk
Two identical portfolios. Same average return. Dramatically different outcomes based on when bad years hit.
The core risk: If markets crash in your first 5 years of retirement, you sell depleted shares to fund spending — leaving less capital to recover. Retiring in 2000 vs 2003 on identical portfolios could mean the difference between running out of money and leaving a $2M legacy.

Mitigation Strategies
Cash Buffer (1-2 years)
Hold 1-2 years of expenses in cash. Avoid selling equities during downturns.
Bond Tent
Increase bond allocation 5 years before and after retirement, then gradually shift back to equities.
Flexible Spending
Reduce withdrawals 10-15% during major downturns. Even small cuts dramatically improve survival rates.
Part-Time Income
Even $10-15K/year in early retirement dramatically reduces sequence risk by cutting portfolio withdrawals.

Inflation-Adjusted FIRE Number
Everything in today's dollars. Your $1.5M FIRE number isn't worth $1.5M in 20 years.
The inflation problem: At 3% inflation, $1.5M today requires $2.47M in 20 years to buy the same things. Most FIRE calculators use nominal numbers. This tool shows you everything in real, today's purchasing power.
