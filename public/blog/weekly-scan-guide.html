The Research Gap Most Retail Investors Have

Wall Street analysts cover the same stocks you're watching. Their research — earnings revision trends, forward multiple compression or expansion, conviction rating changes — moves institutional money before it shows up in price. Retail investors rarely have access to this layer of analysis in real time, and even when they do, parsing raw analyst data across a dozen tickers every week is a multi-hour task that most people simply don't complete.

The result is that retail investors in high-conviction stocks like NVDA, NBIS, MU, or TSM often make buy and hold decisions without understanding the fundamental backdrop that should be driving those decisions. They're trading price action while institutions are trading earnings revisions. That's a structural disadvantage.

The Weekly Fundamental Scan closes that gap. Every Friday, before the weekend, you have a current fundamental snapshot of your most important holdings — so you're walking into Monday with context, not just a chart.

Metrics Covered for Every Ticker

Forward P/E
Price relative to next 12 months earnings estimate. Contextualizes whether you're paying growth or value prices.
PEG Ratio
Forward P/E divided by earnings growth rate. Values above 2 often signal overextension; below 1 signals potential undervaluation.
EV/EBITDA
Enterprise value relative to operating earnings. More meaningful than P/E for capital-intensive companies like datacenters.
FCF Yield
Free cash flow as a percentage of market cap. The cleanest measure of what a business actually returns to shareholders.
Revenue Revision
Week-over-week change in analyst consensus revenue estimates. The single highest-predictive metric in the report.
Analyst Price Target
Consensus target with high and low range — showing current upside or downside to institutional models.

Conviction Ratings and Weekly Thesis

Every ticker receives a 1–5 conviction rating based on a composite of the fundamental metrics, recent momentum, and risk/reward balance. A score of 5 represents a high-conviction position where fundamentals, revisions, and price action are all aligned. A score of 2 or below represents a ticker to reduce or avoid. These ratings change week to week as conditions evolve — and the change is often as informative as the absolute score.

One ticker per week receives a full-length written thesis — a 400–600 word deep dive covering the business model, current catalysts, key risks, and a conviction-rated investment case. This isn't AI-generated content. It's a real thesis written by someone who holds the stock.
